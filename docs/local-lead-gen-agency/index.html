Course Overview & How to Use This

What This Course Is

This is a practical, no-fluff course to help you build a local lead generation agency in India. You will learn to help small businesses — dentists, coaching institutes, gyms, lawyers — get more customers online. In return, they pay you a monthly retainer.

This is a real business model. It takes work. It is not passive income. But the economics are clear: land 5 clients paying ₹10,000/month each and you earn ₹50,000/month from your laptop.

How to Work Through This

  • Read modules 0–2 in 2–3 days. Think hard about the concepts before moving on.
  • Modules 3–5 take 1–2 weeks. Do the actual hands-on work — don't just read.
  • Module 6 onwards: start outreach immediately. Don't wait until you feel "ready." You never will.
  • The 80/20 rule: 20% time learning, 80% time prospecting and talking to potential clients.
The Core Truth You are not selling "digital marketing." You are selling more customers for local businesses. Keep that framing always — it changes how you pitch, price, and deliver.

Mindset & The Reality Check

What Will Actually Happen

Month 1 will feel like nothing is working. You'll send messages, make calls, and hear mostly silence or "not interested." This is normal — it is part of the process, not a sign the model is broken.

  • Most people quit in Month 1 or 2. This is exactly when you must push hardest.
  • Your first client will likely come from your 30th–60th outreach attempt, not your first.
  • One paying client changes everything: proof, confidence, and a case study to show the next prospect.

Skills Beat Credentials

You do not need a marketing degree. Local business owners will judge you on two things: do you understand their problem, and can you show results? That's it. Demonstrable competence beats a certificate every time.

Getting Your First Client for Free

Offer your first 1–2 clients a discounted or free first month in exchange for a testimonial and the right to use their results as a case study. This is not desperation — it's smart strategy. A single case study with real numbers ("18 new patient inquiries in 30 days") is worth more than any portfolio.

Common Mistake Don't work for free for longer than 30 days. You will undervalue yourself and they will undervalue you. Set a clear deadline upfront and transition to a paid plan.

Google Business Profile (GBP) Optimization

Why GBP Is Your Entry-Point Service

GBP is free for businesses, results appear in 4–8 weeks, and most local listings are badly set up. It's the easiest service to sell and deliver — the perfect foot in the door with a new client.

What GBP Optimization Involves

  • Claim & verify — If unclaimed, do so via postcard or phone verification.
  • Business categories — Primary + secondary. Most businesses pick the wrong one.
  • NAP consistency — Name, Address, Phone must match exactly everywhere online.
  • Photos — Upload 15–25 high-quality images: interior, exterior, team, work samples.
  • Business description — Keyword-rich, locally-focused, 250–750 characters.
  • Services/Products section — Fill completely with pricing where relevant.
  • Review strategy — Build a simple system for the owner to ask happy customers for reviews. 20+ reviews with responses = dramatically better ranking.
  • Weekly Google Posts — Offers, updates, news. Keeps the listing active.
  • Q&A section — Add your own Q&As with keywords embedded.
  • Attributes — "Wheelchair accessible," "Accepts cards," etc. Fill everything applicable.

Pricing

Charge ₹5,000–₹15,000 as a one-time setup fee. Offer an optional ₹2,000–₹5,000/month retainer for ongoing management (weekly posts, review responses, monthly reporting).

Meta Lead Generation Ads

How Meta Lead Ads Work

Meta (Facebook + Instagram) Lead Ads show your client's ad to a targeted local audience. When someone clicks, a form pre-fills with their contact details. The lead is captured without leaving the app — fast, low friction, measurable.

Campaign Setup — Step by Step

  1. Access client's Meta Business Suite and their ad account.
  2. Install Meta Pixel on their website if applicable.
  3. Ads Manager → Create → Objective: Leads.
  4. Ad Set: Location (city or 5–10km radius), age range, relevant interests.
  5. Daily budget: ₹200–₹500/day to start and test.
  6. Ad Creative: clear headline, benefit-led body copy, strong CTA ("Book a Free Consultation").
  7. Lead Form: ask for Name, Phone, and one qualifying question only.
  8. Connect leads to Google Sheet via Zapier — or download manually from Meta daily.
  9. Run 2 ad versions simultaneously. Let each spend ₹500 before judging performance.

AI-Powered Lead Follow-up & Automation

Why Follow-up Is Your Biggest Opportunity

Most local businesses generate leads and then do nothing. The owner is busy, there's no system, and a hot lead goes cold within hours. Building an automated follow-up system makes you indispensable — and dramatically improves the results you can show clients.

The Follow-up Tool Stack

  • WhatsApp Business API — Send automated welcome messages the moment a lead comes in.
  • GoHighLevel (GHL) — All-in-one CRM: manage leads, send SMS/email/WhatsApp sequences, book appointments. Start with their 14-day free trial.
  • Zapier or Make — Connect Meta Lead Ads → Google Sheets → WhatsApp/GHL. No code needed. The essential glue for your workflow.
  • ChatGPT — Write all your automation message sequences, email copy, and follow-up scripts in minutes.

A Simple Automation Flow (Start Here)

  1. Lead fills Meta Lead Form
  2. Zapier sends details to Google Sheet + triggers WhatsApp message instantly
  3. WhatsApp: welcome message + what to expect + "Reply YES to confirm your interest"
  4. No reply in 24 hrs → automated follow-up WhatsApp #2
  5. No reply in 48 hrs → SMS or email follow-up
  6. All leads visible in GHL pipeline for the client to track
Bundle This as a Package Position it as: "I don't just get you leads — I build the system to convert them." This justifies higher fees and is the #1 reason clients stay long-term.

Pricing

Charge ₹3,000–₹10,000/month for maintaining the automation and CRM. One-time setup fee of ₹5,000–₹15,000 for the initial build.

How to Present Pricing

Never lead with price. Lead with the result. "I help dental clinics generate 15–30 new patient inquiries per month." When asked the price, frame it as ROI: "At your conversion rate, if even 3 of those leads become patients at ₹5,000 each, that's ₹15,000 from a ₹12,000 investment."

Contracts & Payments

  • Use a simple 3-month service agreement — protects both sides and signals professionalism.
  • Collect first month upfront before starting any work. Non-negotiable.
  • Use Razorpay or UPI for payments.
  • Issue proper invoices using Zoho Invoice (free plan).

Creating Your Agency Identity

  • Pick a simple name. Avoid "Digital," "Solutions," or "Tech" — all overused.
  • Buy a domain (~₹999/year on Namecheap or GoDaddy).
  • Build a one-page website or Notion portfolio — just enough to show you're real.
  • Professional WhatsApp Business account with your agency name and photo.
  • A clean 4-slide PDF proposal on Canva — you'll use this on every call.

Cold Call Scripts by Industry

The 5-Part Call Framework

  1. Permission — "Did I catch you at a bad time?"
  2. Relevance — Show you've specifically researched their business.
  3. Pain question — Ask about their current lead or customer situation.
  4. Implication — Make the gap feel real and costly.
  5. Next step — Book a 15-minute call. You are not selling on this call.

Handling Objections

Objections Are a Good Sign

An objection means they're engaged. A hang-up means they're not. When someone objects, respond with calm curiosity — not a rehearsed rebuttal. The goal is to understand their real concern, not to "win" the argument.

"Not interested."
That's fair. Before I let you go — can I ask one quick question? Are you already getting enough new inquiries each month, or is that something you'd like more of?

If they say "enough": Great, sounds like things are going well. If that ever changes, I'd love to reconnect.
If they hesitate: That's actually the problem I help with. Would 10 minutes this week be worthwhile?
"Send me some information first."
Happy to. To make sure I send something relevant — what's the biggest challenge you're facing right now with getting new customers?

After they answer: Got it. I'll put together something specific to that. What's the best email? And would a quick 10-minute call next week make sense once you've had a look?
"We already have someone handling this."
That's great. I'm not trying to replace anyone. Quick question though — are the results you're getting now meeting your expectations? Are you getting the inquiry volume you hoped for?

If fully satisfied: No worries at all. If not: That's actually why I reached out. Happy to share a second opinion — no commitment.
"I don't have budget for this right now."
Totally understand. Can I ask — if you knew that every ₹10,000 spent would return ₹30,000–₹50,000 in new business, would budget still be the concern?

That's the math I try to show before anyone commits. Would a 10-minute call just to see if the numbers would work for you be fair?
"I've tried Facebook ads before and they didn't work."
I hear that a lot — and honestly, most local business ad campaigns are set up incorrectly. Wrong audience, weak creative, or no follow-up system for the leads that do come in.

I'd love to show you what the difference looks like. Would you be willing to give it 30 days with the right setup?

Closing the Deal & Client Onboarding

The Discovery Call Structure (30 min)

  1. 2 min — Set the agenda: "I'll ask you a few questions, share what I found, and if there's a fit, we can talk next steps."
  2. 10 min — Ask: current marketing, inquiry volume, biggest challenge, what's been tried, what hasn't worked.
  3. 8 min — Present: the specific opportunities you found for their business (GBP gaps, competitor comparison, screenshots).
  4. 5 min — Recommend: one specific package. Don't overwhelm with options.
  5. Close — "Does this seem worth trying for 3 months?"

The Proposal (Keep It Short)

A 4-slide Canva PDF works better than a 20-page document. Include:

  • Page 1: Their specific problem — what you found, with screenshots
  • Page 2: Your recommended solution and how it works
  • Page 3: What results to expect and the timeline
  • Page 4: Pricing and how to get started

Client Onboarding Checklist

  • Collect access: GBP login, Meta Business Manager, website login if needed
  • Sign service agreement before starting any work
  • Collect first month's payment upfront
  • Send a welcome message outlining what to expect in Weeks 1–4
  • Schedule kickoff call to align on goals, audience, and messaging tone
  • Set up shared WhatsApp group for quick communication
  • Create shared Google Sheet for lead tracking
  • Set up monthly report reminder in your own calendar

When Results Are Poor

Results will be poor sometimes. The right response is proactive honesty: reach out before the client does, explain what you think happened, and what you're changing. Clients don't leave because of a bad month — they leave when they feel ignored or blindsided. Communication is your #1 retention tool.
